Gill Scott-Heron

Giving the world forty-one years of spoken words

An American soul and jazz poet, musician and author.
Last known address is New York City where he died after a trip to Europe

Here's a bio timeline of Gill Scott-Heron:

  • 1949: Born in Chicago, Illinois.
  • 1970: After his publication of his book titled, "The Vulture" he began his recording career as a soul poet with the LP Small Talk at 125th and Lenox.
  • 1971: Pieces of a man was released displaying traditional song structured lyrics than loose spoken words.
  • 1972: Third Album was released, entitled, "Free Will."
  • 1974: Much like his previous albums a colaboration LP was released, entitled, "Winter America."
  • 1975: "Midnight Band: The first Minute of a new day" was another joined project with follow musician Brian Jackson. And later that year to protest apartheid in South Africa he released, "Johannasburg."
  • 1976: The live album release of "It's Your World."
  • 1978: The hit single, "Angel Dust" peaked at no. 15 on the R&B charts.
  • 1979: A recording of spoken poetry was released, entitled, "The Mind Of Gill Scott-Heron."
  • 1980-1982 .Four albums were recored and released, "1980", "Real Eyes", "Reflections", and "Moving Target."
  • 1985: Dropped from Arista Records but continured to tour.
  • 1993: Signed a deal with TVT Records and released an album, entitled, "Spirits."
  • 2001: Scott-Heron was sentenced to one to three years imprisonment in a New York State prison for possession of cocaine..
  • 2002: Released from jail and appeared on album, entitled, "Blazing Arrow" by Blackalicious.
  • 2003: Airing of the BBC TV broadcast of the documentary, "The Reveloution Will Not Be Televised."
  • 2006: Scott-Heron was sentenced to two to four years in a New York State prison for violating a plea deal on a drug-possession charge by leaving a drug rehabilitation center..
  • 2008: Gill Scott-Heron confirms rumored that he was HIV positive.
  • 2009: The BBC's Newsnight interviewed Scott-Heron or a feature titled The Legendary Godfather of Rap Returns.
  • 2010: Under critisim Scott-Heron cancels his concert in Isreal. But releases his album, "I'm New Here" on independent label XL Recordings on February 9, 2010.
  • 2011: A remixed album was released, entitled, "We're New Here." Then about a month later Scott-Heron died on the afternoon of May 27, 2011, at St. Luke's Hospital, New York City, after becoming ill upon returning from a European trip.
  • 2014: XL Recordings announced a third album from the I'm New Here sessions, titled Nothing New. The album consists of stripped-down piano and vocal recordings and was released in conjunction with Record Store Day on April 19, 2014.
  • 2015: The cause of Gill Scott-Heron's death was not publicly released.

    • "Gil Scott-Heron released poems as songs, recorded songs that were based on his earliest poems and writings, wrote novels and became a hero to many for his music, activism and his anger. There is always the anger – an often beautiful, passionate anger. An often awkward anger. A very soulful anger. And often it is a very sad anger. But it is the pervasive mood, theme and feeling within his work – and around his work, hovering, piercing, occasionally weighing down; often lifting the work up, helping to place it in your face. And for all the preaching and warning signs in his work, the last two decades of Gil Scott-Heron's life to date have seen him succumb to the pressures and demons he has so often warned others about."

    Fairfax New Zealand, February 2010
